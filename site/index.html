What To Do With A Signal

01

Scan

The bot loads market bars, then each model votes buy, sell, or hold.

02

Require Consensus

A signal only appears when the configured vote threshold is reached.

03

Paper Trade

Use the signal in a simulated account while logging every outcome.

04

Review Results

Automation should wait until backtests and live paper results are consistent.

Buy, Sell, Or Hold

Buy Signal

Enough models agree that price strength, trend, momentum, or mean reversion support a long paper-trade idea.

Sell Signal

Enough models agree risk is rising or downside pressure is stronger than upside evidence.

Hold Signal

There is no strong agreement. The cleanest action is usually to wait instead of forcing a trade.

Terms In Plain English

Consensus

How many independent models agree before the bot treats a signal as meaningful.

Threshold

The minimum votes required. Higher thresholds mean fewer, stricter signals.

Confidence

A signal-quality estimate based on how strongly the vote leans in one direction.

Kelly Size

A suggested paper position size. It is a risk guide, not permission to use real money.

Will This Be Automated?

The current project is designed to produce and log signals first. Fully automated trading should come later, after the bot has enough paper-trading history to prove the rules are stable. Until then, the dashboard should help you decide what to watch, not silently place trades.

  • Paper trading: ready for testing
  • Signal logging: built into the bot
  • Broker automation: wait for proven results
  • Real money: not recommended yet
